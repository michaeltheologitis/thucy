Claim to verify: “NGC 2082 redshift faster than NGC 1705 by 551 kilometer per second plus or minus 6 kilometer per second.”

Summary conclusion:
- The data show NGC 2082 has a recession velocity of 1184 ± 6 km/s and NGC 1705 has 633 ± 6 km/s, so the nominal difference is 551 km/s. However, the dataset does not provide an uncertainty for the difference itself. If the two ±6 km/s uncertainties are independent, standard propagation would give an uncertainty larger than 6 km/s (about ±8.5 km/s). Therefore, the 551 km/s difference is supported, but the ±6 km/s margin on that difference is not supported by the provided data.

Assessment against the claim:
- Supported: NGC 2082 has higher recession velocity than NGC 1705 by 551 km/s (1184 − 633 = 551) according to the dataset.
- Not supported as stated: The dataset lists ±6 km/s for each object individually; it does not specify the uncertainty on the difference. Under typical independent-error propagation, the uncertainty on the difference would be larger than 6 km/s (approximately ±8.5 km/s). Thus, the ±6 km/s uncertainty on the difference is not justified by the provided data.

Assumptions made:
- “Redshift faster” is interpreted as a larger heliocentric recession velocity (km/s) as recorded in the "redshift (km / s )" column.
- The ± values given are 1σ-like uncertainties for each object’s listed velocity. No correlation information is provided in the dataset, so independence is the default assumption when discussing propagated uncertainty.
